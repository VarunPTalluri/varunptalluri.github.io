About me

Hi! I’m Varun, a dual-degree student at the University of Michigan, studying Computer Science in the College of Engineering and Business Administration at the Ross School of Business. I’m passionate about building software that solves real-world challenges, especially at the intersection of technology and business.

My journey into tech started with nine years in competitive robotics, where I focused on autonomous systems and computer vision. Through robotics, I developed a love for the engineering design process and learned the importance of teamwork when tackling complex problems. This passion led me to Ford, where I’ve worked the last two summers, focusing on anomaly detection and machine learning to help improve real-time software decision-making.

On campus, I’m a Teaching Assistant for a robotics course, where I teach scalable solutions for computational linear algebra. I’m also involved in MEG Consulting, where we work with clients ranging from nonprofits to Fortune 500 companies, using data analysis and market research to inform business strategy and drive decision-making.

When I’m not coding or solving problems, you’ll find me playing spikeball, spending time outdoors, or picking up new skills (most recently, juggling)!
